What does the pipeline do?

It ingests files, normalizes their content, splits them into chunks, retrieves relevant context, generates a grounded answer, evaluates the result, and saves a report.

In other words, the pipeline turns raw local documents into a question-answering workflow that can be measured and compared over time.

The important part is that each stage feeds the next one:

- ingestion makes the files usable
- chunking makes them searchable
- retrieval finds the most relevant evidence
- generation turns that evidence into an answer
- evaluation measures whether the answer is good
- reporting stores the result for later comparison

This is the core behavior of the system from end to end.
